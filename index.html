Saibai Island Early Tide Monitoring

We propose a tidal monitoring and early warning system for Saibai Island using solar-powered buoys that collect and transmit sea level data through a long-range wireless network. This system provides a reliable, low-cost, and self-sustaining solution to enhance community safety and disaster preparedness.

[placeholder] The primary pathways by which early warnings propagate from the Bureau of Meteorology (BOM) to Saibai residents utilise the internet and manual communication chains. These pathways have limited redundancy in case of power or telecom outage on Saibai, and limited efficiency in the case of disruptions to the manual communication chain. The Missing Link addresses both of these issues by creating an automatic direct link between the BOM and a radio station in the Torres Strait. By using battery powered radios, Saibai residents can receive early warning broadcasts which bypass any issues with manual communication chains, and which are resistant to power or telecom outages. Design Overview

IoT Sensors

[placeholder]
The Missing Link achieves improved efficiency and redundancy of Saibai’s early warning system by immediately transmitting any warnings on the TSIMA 4MW radio broadcast as soon as they are released by the BOM.
[/placeholder]

Implementation

[placeholder]
The implementation of the design is supported by the BOM’s Registered Users FTP Service, which allows the Missing Link device to receive and broadcast a warning instantaneously when made by the BOM.
[/placeholder]

Components

[placeholder]
The proposal requires a single physical component: a microcomputer, specifically a Raspberry Pi, which will be installed in the TSIMA 4MW radio station studio. This device, the Missing Link, will need a wired connection to the internet and a wired connection to the radio studio’s audio mixing console. The Missing Link will run firmware that pulls alerts from the BOM alert feed, converts the alert’s text to spoken word, and outputs that audio to the mixing console, overriding the current radio broadcast.
[/placeholder]

Users

[placeholder]
Saibai and other Torres Strait Island residents will receive these early warning broadcasts on radio receivers, specifically battery powered radios in the case of power outage. We aim to make residents aware of the improved redundancy and efficiency of the new early warning pathway provided by the Missing Link, which should motivate some residents to monitor their radios, especially during outages.
[/placeholder]
