Saibai Island Early Tide Monitoring

We propose a tidal monitoring and early warning system for Saibai Island using solar-powered buoys that collect and transmit sea level data through a long-range wireless network. This system provides a reliable, low-cost, and self-sustaining solution to enhance community safety and disaster preparedness.

Design Area: 6.1 - ICT: Early Warning Systems

Keywords: Innovation - Resilience - Sustainability

View Proposal Learn More

Proposal Summary

The project aims to address the lack of reliable tidal monitoring and early warning systems on Saibai Island by developing a locally operated, self-sustaining tide monitoring solution. The proposed system consists of solar-powered buoys equipped with linear encoder mechanisms to measure sea level changes and transmit data via a long-range LoRaWAN network. This design provides real-time, accurate tide information to both the Saibai Island community and the Bureau of Meteorology, enabling faster emergency response and improved preparedness during extreme weather events. The system’s modular and low-maintenance nature ensures scalability for other remote island communities facing similar environmental risks.

Design Overview

The tide monitoring buoys measure the height of the tide using a linear encoder mechanism, linked to the shore via the long-range communication standard, LoRaWAN, in order to monitor and deliver accurate data about the height of the tides.

The design includes the following physical components: polyethylene foam body, top mark, indicator light, cast iron deadweight, studless stainless steel chain, shielded twisted pair cable, polycrystalline solar panels, lithium-ion battery, stainless steel tube, stainless steel platform, gears, rotary encoder, esp32, LoRa transceiver.


Data from these buoys will be directly transmitted to the Saibai Island community and relevant authorities, such as the Bureau of Meteorology, to ensure warnings are distributed through accessible community channels if tide levels approach hazardous levels. This is important due to the island’s remote location, limited infrastructure, low elevation and constricted tidal channels which can cause tide heights to vary dramatically over short distances.

Key Considerations

Environmental Factors

  • Built from marine-grade stainless steel and polyethylene foam for corrosion resistance and buoyancy
  • Solar-powered system reduces emissions and maintenance while ensuring continuous operation
  • Artificial reef base enhances marine biodiversity and respects culturally significant areas

Technical Requirements

  • Uses rotary encoder, ESP32, and LoRaWAN for accurate, low-power tide data transmission
  • Solar and battery setup ensures long-term operation with minimal maintenance
  • Watertight housing, redundancy, and remote diagnostics maintain reliability and accuracy

Legislative Challenges

  • Watertight housing, redundancy, and remote diagnostics maintain reliability and accuracy
  • Requires approval and consultation with Torres Strait Regional Authority and Saibai Council
  • Ensures data security through encryption and meets visibility and safety regulations
